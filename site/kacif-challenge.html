CIF Challenge

Grants provided through the Knowledge in Action Career Internship Fund (KACIF) enable students to accept that once-in-a-lifetime internship, instead of having to work a part-time job for financial reasons.

If 500 more donors (1,000 total) make a gift to any area that supports GW students by June 30, they will unlock an ADDITIONAL $50,000 gift to KACIF ($100,000 total). This will allow 60 additional students to take on high-quality, necessarily unpaid internships.

Make your gift today to enable real-world opportunities for students. Your support every year opens doors every day.

During my summer internship with Engineering World Health, I worked in various healthcare settings in Nicaragua to repair damaged medical equipment and identify clinical needs. As one aspect of our job in the Chontales region, we reviewed 75 medical devices including nebulizers, IV pumps, syringe pumps, centrifuges, autoclaves, rotator machines, and more. I observed and experienced so much that will absolutely help me with my career in biomedical engineering and global development. I owe a huge thank you to Engineering World Health, GW Career Services, and my KACIF funders.

Kevin Smith, BA '18

In the summer of 2017, I had the best honor of my life to join a team of world-changers at the United Nations Refugee Agency (UNHCR) regional office in D.C. I was part of the Public Information team, the primary unit responsible for all communications effort between the UNHCR and US media outlets, and on World Refugee Day (June 20), I found myself directly involved in organizing the UNHCR's annual celebration event at the Kennedy Center. The internship at UNHCR has not only led me to discover the intersection of my passion and the need of the world, but it also provided me with further opportunities for professional development. In the Fall 2017 semester, I embarked on my study abroad journey in Jerusalem, while also continuing my service at the United Nations at UNRWA, the United Nations Relief and Works Agency for Palestinian refugees.

Ashley Le, BA '19
